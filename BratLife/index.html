What Happened

This website shares my personal experience with the Brat Life Discord and Reddit communities. Everything here reflects events I lived through, with documentation to support it. This is not harassment, retaliation, or gossip—it is my truth, and I have the right to share it.

At some point staying silent is the problem. At some point looking the other way becomes complicity. And pretending it doesn’t matter because it’s “just Discord” or “just Reddit” becomes a way to protect the people who hurt others without ever being held accountable.

Background

Brat Life is an online kink community that operates both on Reddit (r/BratLife) and through a private Discord server. It was established to provide a space for brat-identified kinksters to connect, share, and express themselves freely within safe boundaries. It attracted a large number of users who were seeking inclusive, emotionally intelligent kink conversation.

What Happened to Me

I was an active member in both the Reddit and Discord communities, participating within their rules. Without any warning or cited rule violation, I was banned from the Brat Life Discord server. No moderator reached out. I later discovered through a former moderator that users were being banned for simply having their own separate Discord servers, regardless of behavior or conduct.

When I attempted to share this experience on Reddit—in a designated venting thread—I was muted for 28 days. My post was not aggressive, harassing, or rule-breaking. It simply told the truth of my experience and the harm it caused. I was later temporarily banned from Reddit as well, under a false harassment claim.

Pattern of Behavior

  • Users being banned from Discord for unrelated community ownership
  • Moderators issuing bans without warning or conversation
  • Reddit mods muting users who express concern or criticism
  • Suppression of valid, non-aggressive personal experiences
  • Former mods confirming the use of bans for control, not behavior

Outcome & Why This Matters

Communities built on power-with rather than power-over require accountability and structure. Silencing users who speak about emotional harm, excluding people for existing outside one community, and punishing dissent without due process erodes psychological safety and discourages vulnerability.

This page is here to tell the truth, invite others to share their experiences, and encourage Brat Life—and similar spaces—to reflect and grow. This is not harassment. It’s documentation and lived experience.
