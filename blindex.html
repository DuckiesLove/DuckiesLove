My Story: Why I Spoke Up

What Happened

This website shares my personal experience with the Brat Life Discord and Reddit communities. Everything here reflects events I lived through, with documentation to support it. This is not harassment, retaliation, or gossip—it is my truth, and I have the right to share it.

At some point staying silent is the problem. At some point looking the other way becomes complicity. And pretending it doesn’t matter because it’s “just Discord” or “just Reddit” becomes a way to protect the people who hurt others without ever being held accountable.

I was banned from the Brat Life Discord server without warning, without explanation, and without ever breaking a rule. A former moderator confirmed the server owner bans people simply for having their own communities. Not for harm. Just perceived threat.

When I shared this on Reddit in r/BratLife, I was muted for 28 days and then temporarily banned for “harassment”—even though I never named anyone or contacted anyone directly. My post was respectful, honest, and rooted in real experience.

If we can’t talk about what happens to us, how do we grow? How do we hold power accountable?
